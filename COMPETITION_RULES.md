# NFL Big Data Bowl 2026 - Competition Rules Summary

This document contains the key competition rules for the NFL Big Data Bowl 2026 Prediction competition on Kaggle. For the most current and authoritative rules, please visit: https://www.kaggle.com/competitions/nfl-big-data-bowl-2026-prediction/rules

## 1. Submission Deadlines & Timeline

- **Competition End Date**: Typically announced at competition launch
- **Final Submission Deadline**: Check competition overview page for exact dates
- **Late Submissions**: Not accepted after the official deadline
- **Leaderboard Freeze**: A period before deadline where submissions are not immediately visible on public leaderboard

---

## 2. Submission Limits

- **Maximum Submissions**: Typically 5-10 submissions per day (exact limit on competition details page)
- **Total Submissions**: Unlimited throughout competition period
- **Daily Reset**: Submission count resets at midnight UTC
- **Evaluation**: Only the final submission before deadline is scored (unless specified otherwise)

---

## 3. Team Requirements

- **Team Size**: Maximum team size typically 5 members
- **Registration**: All team members must have active Kaggle accounts
- **Team Leader**: One member designated as team leader responsible for submissions
- **Late Joiners**: Member additions have restrictions - check current rules
- **Merging**: After certain date, teams cannot merge

---

## 4. Code & Notebook Sharing Requirements

### Before Competition End
- **Requirement**: Make notebooks/code public OR keep private - depends on competition
- **Sharing Deadline**: Code submission deadline (typically end of competition or before)
- **Format**: Kaggle Notebooks, GitHub repositories, or other public platforms acceptable

### After Competition End (if applicable)
- **Disclosure**: May be required to share methodology and code for prize validation
- **Source Control**: Code must be retrievable and reproducible
- **Documentation**: Clear documentation of approach required

---

## 5. Plagiarism & Intellectual Property Rules

- **Original Work**: Submissions must be primarily original work
- **Prohibited**: Copying solutions from previous competitions or other sources
- **Attribution**: External methods/papers must be cited and credited
- **Detection**: Kaggle uses automated and manual plagiarism detection
- **Consequences**: Disqualification from competition and/or Kaggle platform ban
- **Academic Integrity**: For student competitions, university policies may apply

---

## 6. External Data Restrictions

### Allowed Data
- Official competition datasets provided by Kaggle
- Publicly available, non-proprietary datasets
- Datasets already referenced in competition description
- Previously published research datasets

### Prohibited Data
- **Commercial datasets**: Requiring paid licenses
- **Proprietary data**: NFL proprietary data beyond competition datasets
- **Private/confidential data**: Not publicly available
- **Scraped data**: From websites without explicit permission
- **Insider information**: Non-public player/team information
- **Privacy violations**: Personal data of individuals

### Best Practice
- Document all external data sources used
- Ensure compliance with data usage terms
- Disclose data sources in competition discussions if uncertain

## 7. Evaluation Criteria

### Metric
- **Primary Metric**: Specified in competition overview (likely regression or classification metric)
- **Leaderboard Scoring**: Calculated on validation/test set
- **Final Evaluation**: May differ if separate test set used post-deadline

### Leaderboard Types
- **Public Leaderboard**: ~30% of test data (visible throughout)
- **Private Leaderboard**: ~70% of test data (revealed at competition end)
- **Final Ranking**: Based on private leaderboard score

### Special Evaluation Criteria
- **Time Limits**: Code evaluation may have computational constraints
- **Reproducibility**: Solutions may need to be reproducible
- **Documentation**: Clear explanation of approach may be required for prizes

---

## 8. Prize Eligibility & Award Conditions

- **Prize Tiers**: Usually 1st, 2nd, 3rd place, plus honorable mentions
- **Minimum Performance**: May need to meet baseline accuracy threshold
- **Verification**: Prize winners must pass plagiarism and originality checks
- **Grand Prize**: Typically largest for overall competition winner
- **Tier Prizes**: Often smaller amounts for 2nd, 3rd, etc.

## 9. Code of Conduct

- **Respect**: Treat other competitors and organizers with respect
- **No Harassment**: Zero tolerance for harassment or hate speech
- **Legitimate Competition**: No collaboration to artificially inflate scores
- **No Cheating**: Exploiting platform bugs or using unauthorized data
- **Discussion Etiquette**: Discussions should be constructive and helpful
